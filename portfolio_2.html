OVERVIEW

In the modern digital age, understanding the foundational concepts of data, information, and information systems is crucial. Data refers to raw facts and figures, often unprocessed and without context. When this data is organized and given meaning, it transforms into information, enabling better decision-making and insights.

Building on these elements, information systems serve as structured frameworks designed to collect, process, store, and disseminate information. These systems play a pivotal role in managing and optimizing workflows, enabling businesses, organizations, and individuals to make data-driven decisions efficiently.

By exploring the synergy between data, information, and information systems, we can unlock innovative ways to navigate the complexities of our interconnected world.

  • An Executive Information System (EIS) is a decision support software tailored to make decisions as of a high-level executives [1]. It allows for on-demand access, creation, and analysis of information via various computing systems [3]. Executive decisions, often intuitive and broad, take place in an environment of uncertainty, future focus, and limited structure. EIS aids in strategic planning, offers ease of use, and can be customized for individual executives[3]. The one of the difference between DSS to EIS are the following:
    - DSS gives more detailed and specific decisions and is relatively time consuming
    - EIS is for a quick-access broad and summarized decisions
